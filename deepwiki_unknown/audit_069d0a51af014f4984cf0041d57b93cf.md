The `lockSalt` implementation lives in the `PORTAL_LAUNCHER` delegatecall facet, which is not present in the indexed source files. However, the available source is sufficient to resolve this.## Verdict
REJECT

## Rejection Reason

The `SaltAlreadyLockedBySelf` error is explicitly defined with the NatSpec "The caller tried to lock a salt they already hold the lock for," and the `_saltLocks` mapping stores a `SaltLockEntry` with a non-zero `locker` field after the first call. The second `lockSalt` call from the same address on the same salt will hit the `locker == msg.sender` guard and revert — the double-lock is not possible. The question's own local proof plan confirms this: it expects a `SaltAlreadyLockedBySelf` revert on the second call, which is the correct, intended behavior, not a bug.

Even if the guard were somehow absent, the described impact is the attacker losing their own `SALT_LOCK_FEE` — explicitly not fund extraction or reward extraction under the paid-scope definition. No path is shown by which same-address double-locking of the same salt could steal a different user's locked salt, since the `FlapSaltLockedByAnotherUser` check at launch time keys on `locker != msg.sender`, which is unaffected by a same-address re-lock attempt.
