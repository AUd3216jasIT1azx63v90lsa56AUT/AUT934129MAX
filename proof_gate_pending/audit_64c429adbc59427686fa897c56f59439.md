## Verdict
REJECT

## Rejection Reason

The attack premise requires that `recoverStuckDividend` lacks role enforcement in the `PORTAL_TWEAK` delegatecall target. The `Portal.sol` wrapper at lines 463–470 contains no role check itself — it only calls `_delegateToImpl(PORTAL_TWEAK)` — but the role enforcement is documented as residing in the `PORTAL_TWEAK` implementation contract. [1](#0-0) 

The `IPortal.sol` NatSpec at line 2027 explicitly states `"Only callable by AUDITOR_ROLE"` for `recoverStuckDividend`, and the `PORTAL_TWEAK` implementation source is not present in this repository (the repo contains only `Portal.sol`, `PortalBase.sol`, `PortalCommon.sol`, interfaces, and libraries). [2](#0-1) 

Without the `PORTAL_TWEAK` implementation source, the claim that the role check is absent or bypassable is an unverified assumption. The NatSpec affirmatively documents the `AUDITOR_ROLE` guard, and there is no source-level evidence in the available files that the check is missing or circumventable. Promoting this to `NEEDS_LOCAL_PROOF` or higher would require either the `PORTAL_TWEAK` source showing a missing/incorrect `hasRole` check, or a live fork test demonstrating that an unprivileged call succeeds — neither of which is available here. The precondition is speculative, so the finding is rejected per the "prefer REJECT over speculative reports" rule.
