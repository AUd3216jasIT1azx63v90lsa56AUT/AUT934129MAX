## Verdict
REJECT

## Rejection Reason

The actual access-control enforcement for `setTokenBeneficiary` lives inside the `PORTAL_ROLLER` delegatecall target, whose source is **not present in this repository**. The only source available is the thin proxy stub in `src/Portal.sol` lines 351–360, which unconditionally calls `_delegateToImpl(PORTAL_ROLLER)` with no modifier — but that is expected for all roller functions; the guard belongs in the implementation. [1](#0-0) 

The interface NatSpec at `src/interfaces/IPortal.sol` line 1883 explicitly documents the function as **"Allows the default admin to change the beneficiary"**, which is the stated invariant, not evidence of a missing check. [2](#0-1) 

Beyond the missing source, the attack scenario is also logically self-defeating: the hypothetical attacker **is already the current beneficiary**. If they can call `setTokenBeneficiary(T, attacker2)` and then `attacker2` calls `claim(T)`, the net result is the attacker redirecting their **own** future LP-fee entitlement to a second address they control — not extracting value from any other party. The original beneficiary loses nothing they were not already entitled to give away. This is not fund extraction or reward theft from the protocol or other users; it is at most a self-transfer of one's own claimable position, which is expected behavior if the implementation intentionally allows beneficiary self-transfer.

Without the `PORTAL_ROLLER` implementation source to confirm the absence of a role check, and given that the described scenario does not constitute unauthorized extraction even if the call succeeds, the candidate does not meet the bar for `NEEDS_LOCAL_PROOF`.
