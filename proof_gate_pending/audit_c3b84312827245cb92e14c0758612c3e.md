## Rejection Reason

The entire claim rests on the precondition that `PORTAL_TWEAK` lacks role enforcement for `recoverStuckDividend`. The `PORTAL_TWEAK` implementation source is **not present in this repository** — `glob_search` for `**/PortalTweak*` returns zero results. The Portal.sol entry point at lines 463–470 contains no role check and simply calls `_delegateToImpl(PORTAL_TWEAK)`, which is the uniform dispatch pattern for every single function routed through `PORTAL_TWEAK` (`registerExtension`, `setQuoteTokenConfiguration`, `setFeeExemption`, `updateTaxTokenAddresses`, `setFlapFeeProfile`, `setSpammerBlockedBatch`, `excludeAddressFromDividends`, `recoverStuckTaxToken`, `burnStuckTaxToken`, `changeMarketWallet`, `setTokenDividendToken`, `auditorRunIdempotent`). [1](#0-0) 

The design pattern is that all access-control enforcement for `PORTAL_TWEAK` functions lives inside the delegatecall target, not at the Portal.sol dispatch layer. Since `PORTAL_TWEAK` executes via `delegatecall`, any `hasRole(AUDITOR_ROLE, msg.sender)` check inside it reads Portal's own AccessControl storage, which is the correct and expected mechanism. [2](#0-1) 

Without the `PORTAL_TWEAK` source, the claim that the role check is absent or bypassable is a pure assumption. The `AUDITOR_ROLE` is referenced 10 times in `IPortal.sol`, strongly suggesting it is documented and enforced in the implementation. Promoting this to even `NEEDS_LOCAL_PROOF` would require source-level evidence that the check is missing — which is not available here. Per the rules, speculative source-only theories without a concrete reachable path must be rejected. [3](#0-2)
