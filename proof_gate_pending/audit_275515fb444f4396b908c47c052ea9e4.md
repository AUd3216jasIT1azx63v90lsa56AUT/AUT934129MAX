## Verdict
REJECT

## Rejection Reason

The hypothesis is architecturally unsound. `_delegateToImpl(PORTAL_TWEAK)` is a `delegatecall`, which preserves `msg.sender` and executes in Portal's own storage context. If `PORTAL_TWEAK` contains `_checkRole(AUDITOR_ROLE)` (as the question's own premise states), that check runs against Portal's `AccessControl` storage with the original caller as `msg.sender` — exactly as if the check were in the dispatcher itself. There is no bypass: the role check in the delegatecall target is the enforcement mechanism, not a gap created by the dispatcher omitting a pre-check.

The PORTAL_TWEAK implementation source is not present in the indexed repository  — only `Portal.sol`, `PortalBase.sol`, `PortalCommon.sol`, interfaces, and libraries are available — so the actual body of `recoverStuckTaxToken` in PORTAL_TWEAK cannot be read. However, the question's own precondition ("the AUDITOR_ROLE check is in the PORTAL_TWEAK delegatecall target") describes the correct and secure pattern, not a vulnerability. The interface NatSpec unambiguously documents the restriction [1](#0-0) , and `Portal.sol` dispatches without a pre-check as expected for a facet/module architecture [2](#0-1) .

A real finding would require demonstrating that the PORTAL_TWEAK implementation is **missing** the role check entirely — which is the opposite of the stated precondition and cannot be confirmed from available source. Speculating that the check is absent without source evidence, after the premise explicitly states it is present, does not meet the bar for even NEEDS_LOCAL_PROOF.
